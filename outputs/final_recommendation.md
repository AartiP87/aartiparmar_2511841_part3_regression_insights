# Final Recommendation

## Executive Summary

The purpose of this analysis was to identify the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales performance across retail stores and provide evidence-based recommendations to management.

The final multiple regression model achieved an **R-squared value of 0.7890**, indicating that approximately **78.9% of the variation in monthly sales** can be explained by the variables included in the model.

The analysis suggests that customer traffic, marketing investment, and discount strategy are the most important drivers of sales performance.

---

# Factors Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales

Based on the regression results, the variables most strongly associated with monthly sales are:

## 1. Footfall

* Coefficient: **29.3469**
* p-value: **< 0.001**
* Direction: Positive

### Interpretation

Each additional customer visiting the store is associated with approximately **AED 29.35** in additional monthly sales.

This is one of the strongest predictors of sales performance.

---

## 2. Marketing Spend

* Coefficient: **1.1145**
* p-value: **< 0.001**
* Direction: Positive

### Interpretation

Every additional AED 1 invested in marketing is associated with approximately **AED 1.11** in additional monthly sales.

Marketing investment appears to contribute positively to store performance.

---

## 3. Average Discount Percentage

* Coefficient: **-81,407.12**
* p-value: **0.044**
* Direction: Negative

### Interpretation

Higher discount levels are associated with lower monthly sales after controlling for other factors.

This suggests that excessive discounting may reduce overall revenue and should be managed carefully.

---

# Variables Leadership Should Focus On

Management should prioritize the following areas:

## Increase Customer Footfall

Customer traffic is the strongest predictor of sales performance.

Recommended actions:

* Increase customer acquisition campaigns.
* Implement loyalty and retention programs.
* Improve in-store experience.
* Invest in local marketing activities.

---

## Optimize Marketing Investment

Marketing spend has a strong positive relationship with monthly sales.

Recommended actions:

* Continue investing in high-performing marketing channels.
* Measure marketing return on investment regularly.
* Allocate budgets toward campaigns that generate store traffic.

---

## Review Discount Strategy

The analysis suggests that aggressive discounting may negatively impact sales performance.

Recommended actions:

* Use targeted promotions rather than blanket discounts.
* Focus on value creation instead of price reductions.
* Evaluate the profitability of discount campaigns.

---

# Risks and Limitations

Although the model performs well, several important variables are not included:

* Competitor activity
* Economic conditions
* Customer demographics
* Store size
* Product assortment
* Seasonality
* Store management quality
* Local market conditions

These factors may also influence monthly sales performance.

---

# Why Regression Does Not Prove Causation

Regression analysis identifies **statistical associations** between variables and monthly sales.

However, it does **not automatically prove cause and effect**.

For example:

* Higher marketing spend may be associated with higher sales because successful stores have larger budgets.
* Stores with higher footfall may also benefit from better locations or stronger brand recognition.

Therefore, management should use regression results as **decision-support evidence** rather than proof that one variable directly causes changes in sales.

The regression results provide valuable insights into the factors associated with sales performance and can support future planning, budgeting, and operational decision-making.
